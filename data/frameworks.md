# Reflective Practice Frameworks

Three pedagogical frameworks used to generate age-appropriate reflection questions.

## What? So What? Now What?

For younger learners (under 12). Three simple stages: describe what happened, why it matters, and what to do next. Questions should be warm, short, and use simple language.

## Gibbs Reflective Cycle

For older learners (12+). Six stages: description, feelings, evaluation, analysis, conclusion, and action plan. Each stage builds on the previous one and encourages deeper thinking.

## Kolb Experiential Learning Cycle

For ages 10–14, especially hands-on activities. Four stages: concrete experience, reflective observation, abstract conceptualization, and active experimentation. Connects doing with understanding.

## How They're Used

The backend selects the framework based on the learner's age. The model receives the framework and stage as part of its input and generates a question accordingly.
